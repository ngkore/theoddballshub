Another short one, because this message doesn't need 2,000 words: **take care of your
body now, while it's easy.**

## Why it's easy to ignore

In your 20s, you can get away with almost anything — bad sleep, no exercise, eating
whatever's convenient — and feel mostly fine. That's exactly the trap. The bill doesn't
arrive immediately, but it does arrive, usually as a habit that's much harder to build
once your body stops being so forgiving.

## The basics, and that's genuinely it

- **Move, regularly.** Doesn't have to be a fancy gym membership — walking, a home
  workout, sports, anything that gets you moving most days. Consistency beats
  intensity by a mile.
- **Eat like someone who plans to use this body for another 50 years.** More whole
  food, less stuff that comes in a wrapper, and water instead of your third sugary
  drink of the day. Nobody's asking for perfect.
- **Sleep isn't optional.** It's the cheapest, most effective recovery tool you have,
  and skipping it undoes a lot of the other effort.

## What we'd tell a friend

Pick one small thing and make it boring and automatic — a short walk after dinner, one
home-cooked meal a day, lights off by a set time. Don't wait for a health scare or a
new year to "start." The version of you a few years from now is built entirely out of
what you repeat today.

*General wellness reminder, not medical advice — talk to a doctor for anything specific
to your health.*
