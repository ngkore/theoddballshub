When we started, the Prague long-stay process looked like a wall of forms. It's not
actually that bad — it's just that nobody lays out the *order*. Here's the version we
wish we'd had.

## Before you touch an application

Get these in one folder, because every later step asks for them:

- A passport valid well past your intended stay
- Proof of purpose (admission letter, work contract, etc.)
- Proof of accommodation in Czechia
- Proof of funds

> The single biggest delay we hit wasn't the embassy — it was a document from back home
> arriving late. Start the slow paperwork **first**, before you book anything.

## The order that actually works

1. Lock your purpose document (school or employer).
2. Sort accommodation proof — even a short initial lease counts.
3. Gather funds proof and get translations done early.
4. Book the consulate appointment (these fill up; book as soon as you're eligible).
5. Submit, then wait — and don't make non-refundable travel plans around an optimistic date.

## What we'd tell a friend

Build in a buffer of a few weeks you don't think you'll need. You'll need it. The people
who had a smooth time weren't lucky — they just started the boring documents a month
earlier than felt necessary.

*This is our experience, not legal advice — rules change, so check the current official
requirements for your situation.*
