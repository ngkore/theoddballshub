If there's one piece of money advice we wish someone had drummed into us at 22, it's
this: **start a SIP, even a small one, as early as possible.** Not when you're earning
"enough." Not after the next raise. Now.

## What is a SIP?

A **SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)** is just a fixed amount of money that gets
automatically invested — usually into a mutual fund — on a set date every month. You
set it up once, link it to your bank account, and it runs on its own. ₹500, ₹2,000,
₹10,000, whatever you can spare — same idea, different scale.

That's it. No timing the market, no checking prices every day, no drama. It's the
"set it and forget it" version of investing.

## Why early beats big

The honest reason SIPs work isn't magic returns — it's **time** and **compounding**.
Compounding is just your returns earning their own returns, year after year. The longer
your money is in, the more of the growth comes from the market doing the work, not from
you adding more.

Here's the part that actually motivates people once it clicks: someone who starts a
modest SIP at 23 and someone who starts a much larger one at 35 — assuming similar
returns — the early starter very often ends up ahead, having put in *less* total money,
simply because their money had a decade more to grow.

> The cost of waiting one year isn't "one year of contributions." It's one year of
> compounding lost off the *end* — which, for long horizons, tends to be where most of
> the growth actually happens.

## Why it actually works for most people

- **It removes the "right time" problem.** Markets go up and down; a SIP buys at all
  of those points, averaging it out instead of betting on one moment.
- **It builds a habit before it builds wealth.** Treating investing like a recurring
  bill — happening automatically, before you can spend the money elsewhere — is the
  single biggest predictor of whether people actually stick with it.
- **It scales with you.** Start small while you're a student or just starting out;
  increase the amount as your income grows. The habit is the hard part, not the
  amount.

## What we'd tell a friend

You don't need to "figure out investing" before you start — that's how years slip by.
Pick a well-known, low-cost index fund, start a small SIP today, and learn the rest
*while* it's already running. The amount matters far less than the **starting date**.

The version of you ten years from now isn't going to care that you started with ₹500.
They're going to care that you started.

*This is general, educational information based on our own experience — not financial
advice. Returns aren't guaranteed, and you should understand a fund (and ideally talk
to a qualified advisor) before investing.*
