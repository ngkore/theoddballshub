A friend of ours applied for a Master's at the **Czech University of Life Sciences
Prague (CZU)** — Faculty of Economics and Management. The process itself isn't
complicated, but it's easy to lose months if you do things in the wrong order. This is
the distilled version of what we learned, written generally enough to be useful for
most CZU master's programmes (Economics & Management, Business Administration, Global
Information Security Management, International Trade and Business, Informatics,
European Agrarian Diplomacy — check the specifics for your own programme).

> If you're applying to a *different* Prague university, the document-recognition piece
> below (nostrification / apostille) applies to you too — see our separate post on that.

## The one thing that decides everything

Before anything else gets issued — acceptance letter, visa, anything — your **foreign
degree has to be recognised** by the university. This is called **Nostrification**
(or, for CZU specifically, an **Internal Evaluation** as a one-year alternative). It
involves an apostille on your degree documents, which can take weeks depending on your
home country.

**Start the apostille process the same week you decide to apply.** This single delay is
the most common reason people miss the intake entirely. We have a full breakdown of
this in our nostrification post.

## Application timeline (September intake)

| When | What happens |
|---|---|
| October – 31 March | Online application open at `is.czu.cz`. Application fee ~850 CZK (non-refundable). |
| Nov – March | Invitations for the online entrance exam go out on a rolling basis (roughly every few weeks) as your application becomes complete — the earlier you apply, the earlier you get a slot. One attempt only. |
| End of March | Application submitted **and** degree-recognition documents already in progress |
| End of April | Degree recognition completed, tuition fee paid, second/final round of dormitory requests |
| Late April / early May | Acceptance letter issued (sent as a single hard copy by courier — see note below) |
| May – mid-June | Embassy/visa appointment |
| September | Arrival in Prague, registration |

**Miss the end-of-April deadlines and the admission gets cancelled for that intake** —
there's no fast-tracking it, you'd have to wait a full year.

> **The acceptance letter is sent by courier exactly once.** Before you ask for it,
> make sure everything that should travel with it — your dormitory confirmation, any
> visa-nomination paperwork — is actually ready. There's no second shipment if you
> forget something.

## The entrance exam

- Held online (proctored — camera and mic on, one screen, no notes/phone/AI tools).
- For Economics & Management: covers Economics & Management plus Quantitative
  Methods, 50 points each.
- English is assessed as part of the exam, so a separate language certificate usually
  isn't required — but double-check this for your specific programme.
- You get **one attempt**. Don't sit it half-prepared just to "see the format."

## Fees, roughly

- Application fee: ~850 CZK
- Nostrification fee: ~3,000 CZK (or ~900 CZK for Internal Evaluation, CZU-specific)
- Tuition: noted as around 50,000 CZK per year for the programme we followed — paid in
  one lump sum, **before** the acceptance letter is issued. Refunds are only possible if
  the visa is refused, recognition is rejected, or no embassy appointment could be
  secured (a cancellation fee is deducted).

*Tuition varies significantly by programme — always confirm on the faculty's own page.*

## Visa and accommodation, in brief

- You need the **acceptance letter by end of May at the latest** to have a realistic
  shot at a visa appointment before September — visa processing itself can take up to
  three months, so the embassy appointment should ideally happen by mid-June.
- If you want a dormitory (often needed as proof of accommodation for the visa), there
  are two application rounds — roughly one after your entrance exam (around
  February) and a second one in April. After 1 July, CZU stops issuing confirmations
  entirely, so don't leave this until the summer.
- Some applicants may be eligible for a fast-track visa appointment process (sometimes
  called "Student Regime") if their degree was completed within the last 3 years —
  the university nominates eligible students directly, but only once everything else
  (degree recognition, tuition, accommodation) is sorted, so it's worth asking about
  early rather than assuming it'll happen automatically.

## The embassy visa interview

Once you have your acceptance letter, the embassy appointment is its own hurdle —
plenty of people get this far and then stumble at the interview because they treat it
as a formality. It isn't. We've written a [separate post on what to bring and how to
prepare](czech-student-visa-embassy-appointment/), including the document
checklist.

## Who to contact, and how

- Degree/recognition matters go to the **Recognition Office** — not the programme
  coordinator.
- Programme/admissions questions go to the **study programme coordinator** for your
  specific course.
- Don't send original or notarised documents anywhere without getting email
  confirmation first.

## What we'd tell a friend (because we did)

Do the boring, slow thing first — the apostille — even before you're sure about the
exam or the programme details. Everything else can move fast once that's in motion;
nothing else can move at all until it is.

*This is based on our own experience helping with one application — programmes,
deadlines and fees change year to year, so always check the university's current
official pages before relying on any of this.*
