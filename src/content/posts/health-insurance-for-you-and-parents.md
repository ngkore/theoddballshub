This one's short on purpose, because the message is simple: if you don't already have
**health insurance** — for yourself, and separately for your parents — get it. Soon.

## Why it matters more now

Healthcare costs have been climbing faster than most people realise, and a single
hospitalisation can wipe out months (or years) of savings in a few days. Most of us
in our 20s feel invincible — until we're not, or until it's a parent who needs care.

## Why "now," specifically

- **Premiums are cheaper when you're younger and healthier.** The same cover costs
  more every year you wait, and a new health condition later can make it harder (or
  pricier) to get covered at all.
- **Your parents' cover can't wait for "later."** Premiums for older age groups are
  significantly higher, and pre-existing conditions can limit what's covered — the
  earlier you get them insured, the better the terms lock in.
- **One bad year can undo years of saving.** That SIP you just started? A medical
  emergency without insurance can wipe it out overnight. Insurance is what protects
  the rest of your financial plan.

## What we'd tell a friend

You don't need the most expensive plan — you need *a* plan, in place, before you need
it. Start with a basic individual or family floater for yourself, and a separate
senior-citizen plan for your parents if they don't already have one. Compare a couple
of options, read what's excluded, and get it sorted this month, not "eventually."

It's one of those things that feels unnecessary right up until the day it isn't —
and by then, it's too late to buy it.

*General awareness, not financial or insurance advice — compare plans and read the
policy terms carefully before buying.*
